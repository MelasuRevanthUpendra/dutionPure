Oil Trap System

Nearly half a million tonnes of fat, oil abd grease enter into drainage system. Grease sticks to pipe walls, which can eventually lead to blockage while fats and oils damage waste water treatment equipment, which leads to repairs. If allowed to enter a natural water resources, fats, oils and grease (FOG) can cause serious damage to the downtime.

For these reasons, all industries should ensure that the correct FOG management is used by them. FOG causes big problems in commercial kitchen's, causing blockages in internal pipes leading to expensive repairs and potential equipment downtime.

What is Oil trap System?

Oil trap system is device used to trap fat, oil and grease from waste water before it enters into the drainage system.

It prevents the cogging of sewer by preventing the oil, grease, fats and solids from entering the drainage.

Commonly used in Hostels, Restaurants, Banquet Halls, Food Processing Establishments, commercial kitchens, hospitals, etc..

How does Oil trap system works?

Fats and Oils are 10-15% less dense than water and also it doesn't mixup with water. It normally floats on the top of water.

Oil trap system has 3 layers. When waste water enters into first layer the oil gets floats up in the water. Then the remaining water and suspended solids move into next layer. Solids settle at the bottom and the remainng water moves next.After all solids and waste gets cleared, the seperated clear water excapes under an outlet.

Over time, solids and grease build-up, and if eft to accumulate for long enough they can start to escape through the outlet and in some circumstances, they can back-up through the inlet. For this reason, the trap must be cleaned / pumped out on a regular basis.

Time taken to clean the trap will depend on the wastewater produced and size of the trap system but usually it takes 2-4 weeks. This period also lengthened up to 8 weeks by adding a biological grease treatment fluid into the system. The solution combines non-pathogenic bacteria with nutrients and enzymes to break down FOG, aiding grease trap performance. This process is commonly referred to as "dosing".

Dosing can also be implemented at the outlet stage as a further method of preventing FOG build-up in internal piping.
